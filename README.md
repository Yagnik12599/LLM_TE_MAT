# Large Language Model-Assisted Discovery of Optimal Dopants for Enhanced Thermoelectric Performance in CoSb₃-Based Skutterudites<img width="468" height="83" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c61547da-265f-4b2f-9fc3-a5c06e7e4140" />
We present a data-driven approach for accelerating the discovery of high-performance CoSb₃-based skutterudites by curating a comprehensive dataset of compositions with various filler elements from over 300 research articles. Leveraging large language models (LLMs), we extract and embed compositional representations, which are then used to train a regression model for predicting thermoelectric constants. Compared to traditional deep neural networks relying on elemental descriptors such as atomic radii, our LLM-based model achieves significantly lower mean-squared error (MSE) losses. We further employ the trained model coupled with random sampling to propose novel filler compositions with promising thermoelectric properties. Finally, we validate these predicted candidates through first-principles density functional theory (DFT) calculations to assess their electrical and thermal conductivity. This integrated framework demonstrates the potential of combining natural language processing, machine learning, and quantum simulations for thermoelectric materials design.<img width="468" height="359" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/01c8f325-baea-4caa-8b49-f97753d28cb1" />
